Our Research

Decoding cancer's complexities through multi-omics, computational biology, and targeted therapeutics.

Key Research Areas

We investigate how alterations in the NRF2 pathway contribute to cancer development and drug resistance. Our work aims to understand its role in tumor metabolism and develop novel strategies to target NRF2-driven cancers effectively.

Our lab integrates genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic data to discover novel biomarkers. We analyze a wide array of genetic information—including mutations, CNVs, and fusions—and apply machine learning models to predict cancer progression and therapy response.

A core focus is identifying clinically actionable mutations to guide personalized treatment strategies. We develop AI-driven models for patient stratification and explore novel drug combinations to create more effective, targeted therapies.

We study the complex interactions between tumors and the immune system to understand immune evasion mechanisms. Our research includes screening small molecules and repurposed drugs to discover new therapies and studying combination approaches to overcome resistance.
